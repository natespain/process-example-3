The Design Challenge

What is the Design Challenge?

What is the problem you're trying to solve?

Help the homeless and really poor in Lakeland.

Take a stab at framing it as a design question.

How can Lighthouse Ministries tell the homeless of their aid?

Now state the ultimate impact you're trying to have.

Let the homeless know of the housing and education options at Lighthouse Ministries.

What are some possible solutions to your problem? Think broadly. It's fine to start a project with a hunch or two, but make sure you allow for surprising outcomes.

It needs to have a clear goal and volunteer opportunities on the home page. It needs to give specific options clearly. It should outline the programs they offer. A new color scheme should be chosen.

Finally, write down some of the context and constraints that you're facing. They could be geographic, technological, time-based, or have to do with the population you're trying to reach.

This is not in Anderson, that could be an issue. There is a very broad range of assistance. There are two main audiences: need help and give help.

Does you original questions need a tweak? Try it again.

How can the Lighthouse Ministries website clearly show the variety of help it offers while recieving two streams of viewers: those who want to give and those who need to get.
